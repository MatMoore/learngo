How to play Go

Go is a 2-player strategy game with a few simple rules.

One player takes black stones, and the other takes white. The black player always goes first, unless they are playing the game with a handicap.

In this interactive tutorial, you'll be playing as black. Professional Go is played on a 19x19 grid, but to learn the rules, we'll use a smaller 9x9 grid.

Take turns to place a stone on the board

In Go, both players take turns to place a stone on the board.

Unlike chess, you place stones on the points where the lines cross, not in the spaces between them.

Each empty point next to a stone is one "liberty"

Each point on the board is connected by lines to 2, 3 or 4 other points (depending if it is a corner, side, or middle point). If any of those connecting points are empty, each one counts as one liberty for that stone.

You can take away liberties of your opponent's stone by placing your stones on those points.

When an opponent's stone has no liberties unfilled, you've captured it, and you remove that stone from the board.
